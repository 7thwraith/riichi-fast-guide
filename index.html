Completed Mahjong Hands

Must consist of the following

  • A ready hand

    One tile away from winning.

  • The winning tile

    Normally the 14th tile addition to the thirteen (13) tiles in your hand.

  • A Yaku ⚠

    A condition to satisfy

Setup: Seating

Decide on who the dealer is.

Seat Assignment

  • The dealer is always assigned the East seat.
  • The player opposite of dealer is always assigned the West seat.
  • The player left of dealer is always assigned the North seat.
  • The player right of dealer is always assigned the South seat.

Dealership

  • If dealer at current turn is unable to (1) declare a win OR (2) have a ready hand, dealership passes to the player to the right of the dealer.

Setup: Rounds

Decide if playing one round or two rounds.

Round Completion

  • A round completes when everyone has had a turn being a dealer.

Names of Rounds

First round is called East Round. Second round is called South Round.

Gameplay

During Your Turn

  1. Draw one (1) tile.
  2. Check to see if drawn tile completes your winning hand.

    • If yes:

      declare your win by calling TSUMO and revealing your hand. The round ends.

    • If no:

      discard any one (1) tile. The turn ends.

During Opponent's Turn, After a Discard

  • All of the following are optional. If discarded tile can complete...

    • A Hand

      you may declare your win by calling RON and revealing your hand. The round ends.

    • A Meld

      you may "call" the tile if permissible (see below), take the discarded tile, and reveal the completed meld. The turn ends.

    A discarded tile may simultaneously be eligible to complete both a hand or a meld in your hand, but you can only choose one or the other.
    Calls on a Discard
  • When taking a discarded tile to complete a meld, you need to call the following

  • Pon:

    Complete a Triplet

  • Kan:

    Complete a Quad

  • Chi:

    Complete a Sequence. Only allowed when tile is discarded by player to your left AND nobody else has called the tile.
